3. Essential Road Rules in Kenya

Speed Limits

  • Urban areas: 50 km/h
  • Rural roads: 80 km/h
  • Highways: 100 km/h
  • School zones: 30 km/h
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Speed limits may vary based on road conditions and signage. Always observe posted limits.

Right of Way

  • Vehicles on main roads have priority over side roads
  • At roundabouts, give way to traffic from the right
  • Pedestrians at zebra crossings have absolute priority
  • Emergency vehicles with sirens/lights have priority

Lane Discipline

  • Keep left except when overtaking
  • Use indicators before changing lanes
  • Check blind spots before lane changes
  • Maintain safe following distance (3-second rule)
üí° Pro Tip: The 3-second rule - Pick a fixed point ahead. When the vehicle in front passes it, count "one thousand and one, one thousand and two, one thousand and three". You should not reach that point before finishing the count.

5. Pre-Drive Safety Checks (POWDERY)

Perform these checks before every journey:

  • P - Petrol/Fuel: Check fuel level is adequate
  • O - Oil: Check engine oil level
  • W - Water: Check coolant and windscreen washer fluid
  • D - Damage: Walk around vehicle checking for damage
  • E - Electrics: Test all lights and indicators
  • R - Rubber: Check tire pressure and tread depth
  • Y - Yourself: Ensure you're fit to drive
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Never drive if you're tired, unwell, or under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

6. Emergency Procedures

Brake Failure

  • Pump the brake pedal rapidly
  • Use engine braking (downshift gears)
  • Apply handbrake gradually
  • Steer to safe area if possible

Tire Blowout

  • Grip steering wheel firmly
  • Do NOT brake suddenly
  • Ease off accelerator gradually
  • Steer straight, then move to roadside

Skidding

  • Steer in direction of skid
  • Do NOT brake hard
  • Ease off accelerator
  • Wait for traction to return
üí° Remember: In emergencies, stay calm and follow your training. Panic causes mistakes.
