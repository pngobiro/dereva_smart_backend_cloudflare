1. Parallel Parking

The most challenging parking maneuver - essential for urban driving and your test.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Pull alongside the front car, leaving 30cm gap
  2. Align your side mirror with their rear bumper
  3. Put car in reverse, check all mirrors
  4. Turn steering wheel fully right
  5. Reverse slowly until 45¬∞ angle
  6. Straighten wheel, continue reversing
  7. Turn wheel fully left when rear is in
  8. Straighten up, center in space

Common Mistakes

  • ‚ùå Starting too far from front car
  • ‚ùå Turning steering wheel too early
  • ‚ùå Reversing too fast
  • ‚ùå Not checking blind spots
  • ‚ùå Hitting the kerb
  • ‚ùå Ending up too far from kerb
üí° Pro Tip: Practice makes perfect! The key is smooth, slow movements and constant observation. Use your mirrors more than looking back.

2. Bay Parking (90¬∞ Parking)

Forward and reverse bay parking for shopping centers and parking lots.

Forward Bay Parking

  1. Approach slowly, indicator on
  2. Position car 1 meter from bay
  3. When front wheel aligns with bay line, turn wheel
  4. Drive forward into bay
  5. Straighten wheel as you enter
  6. Center between lines

Reverse Bay Parking

  1. Drive past bay, align mirror with bay line
  2. Stop, engage reverse
  3. Check all around for pedestrians
  4. Reverse slowly, turn wheel when aligned
  5. Guide car into bay using mirrors
  6. Straighten up, center in bay
‚ö†Ô∏è Safety First: Always check for pedestrians, especially children, before reversing. Use your mirrors and look over your shoulder.

3. Angle Parking (45¬∞ Parking)

Common in parking lots with angled bays - easier than 90¬∞ parking.

Technique

  1. Approach at slow speed
  2. Signal your intention
  3. Position car in center of lane
  4. When front of car reaches bay, turn wheel
  5. Follow the angle of the bay
  6. Straighten wheel, center in space
  7. Leave equal space on both sides
‚úÖ Advantage: Angle parking is easier to enter and exit than 90¬∞ bays. It's also safer as you can see oncoming traffic when leaving.

4. Hill Start & Parking on Slopes

Special techniques for parking on inclines and declines.

Parking Uphill

  • Turn wheels AWAY from kerb
  • If car rolls back, it hits kerb and stops
  • Apply handbrake firmly
  • Leave in 1st gear (manual)

Parking Downhill

  • Turn wheels TOWARDS kerb
  • If car rolls forward, kerb stops it
  • Apply handbrake firmly
  • Leave in reverse gear (manual)

Hill Start Technique

  1. Handbrake on, foot on brake
  2. Select 1st gear
  3. Set gas to 1500-2000 RPM
  4. Find clutch biting point
  5. Release handbrake smoothly
  6. Increase gas, release clutch
